Text to Speech Models

Tacotron2

Tacotron2 maps character embeddings to mel-scale spectrograms using a recurrent sequential feature prediction network. From those spectrograms, a tweaked WaveNet model acts as a vocoder, generating time-domain waveforms.

Deep Voice3

DeepVoice 3 is the oldest of the three models being compared in the research. It is a fully convolutional and sequential neural TTS system. Deep Voice 3 uses residual convolutional layers to encode words into per-timestep key and value vectors for an attention-based decoder.

Fast Speech2

The FastSpeech2 model takes considerably less time for training than the other two models. It requires a much larger number of inputs. It incorporates an auto-regressive instructor model, which assists the system in producing different voice variations that correlate to the actual text.

Our Findings

Models Mean Opinion Score
Tacotron2 4.25 ± 0.17
DeepVoice 3 1.90 ± 0.43
FastSpeech2 3.93 ± 0.19
Ground Truth 4.62 ± 0.14

In this research, we were successful in training and fine-tune three speech synthesis models with low dataset duration and on a decently powerful GPU. Our results provide a clear comparative analysis of the exiting TTS models and it paves the way to testing various complex systems in low-resource environments.
From the mean opinion scores it can be extrapolated that Tacotron 2 had the closest flow and pronunciation to the ground truth and Deep Voice 3 performed the worst amongst the three models. Additionally, in our testing we found that training and tuning Tacotron 2 model is far more complex than training FastSpeech 2 model.
